## 📌 LEGAL PRECEDENT: Merck Frosst Canada Ltd. v. Canada (Attorney General)

### Case Details
- **Citation:** 2011 FCA 327
- **Court:** Federal Court of Appeal
- **Outcome:** Federal Court of Appeal ordered disclosure of pharmaceutical contract pricing
- **Key Holding:** Public interest in transparency overrides commercial confidentiality

### Key Legal Principles
1. **Presumption of Disclosure** — Government documents should be disclosed unless specific exemption applies
2. **Public Interest Override** — Commercial confidentiality does not override public interest in accountability
3. **Contract Terms Disclosable** — Pricing and terms of government contracts are not protected by cabinet confidence

### Legal Arguments

#### 1. Presumption of Disclosure
- Government documents are presumed to be disclosable
- Burden is on government to justify refusal
- *Canada (Information Commissioner) v. Canada (Minister of National Defence), 2011 SCC 25*

#### 2. Public Interest Override
- Public interest in transparency outweighs commercial confidentiality
- Contracts involving public funds are subject to disclosure
- *Merck Frosst Canada Ltd. v. Canada (Attorney General), 2011 FCA 327*

#### 3. No Cabinet Confidence
- Contract terms are not cabinet confidences
- Commercial contracts are not protected by cabinet privilege
- *Canada v. Minister of Defence, 2018 FCA 131*

#### 4. Charter Right to Information
- Section 2(b) Charter includes right to receive government information
- Section 4(b) Constitution requires parliamentary accountability
- *Canadian Broadcasting Corp. v. Canada (Attorney General), 2011 SCC 3*
