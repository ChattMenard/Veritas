## 📊 EVIDENCE OF OBSTRUCTION

### Constitutional Requirement
- Section 4(b) of the Constitution requires Parliament to be summoned at least once every 12 months
- Written questions are a parliamentary mechanism for ministerial accountability
- Responses must be "complete and accurate" (Parliamentary rules)

### Violation Indicators
1. **8-month response time** exceeds reasonable expectation
2. **Inaccessible content** violates presumption of openness
3. **Coordinated delay** suggests intentional obstruction
4. **No justification** provided for content inaccessibility

## 🎯 LEGAL PRECEDENTS

### Canada (House of Commons) v. Vaid, 2005 SCC 30
- Supreme Court affirmed parliamentary privilege
- Parliament has exclusive jurisdiction over its internal proceedings
- However, this does not justify withholding responses from the public

### Information Commissioner v. Canada (Minister of National Defence), 2011 SCC 25
- Supreme Court affirmed presumption of disclosure
- Government must justify withholding information
- Delay tactics are not acceptable
